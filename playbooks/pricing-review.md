# Playbook: Pricing Review

**Invoke:** Before any pricing change or new pricing tier launch
**Specialists involved:** Finance, Marketing, Legal
**Owner:** Founder (decides)

## Steps

1. **Finance:** Current unit economics — cost per MAU, AI API cost per feature use, revenue per user
2. **Marketing:** Competitive pricing landscape — what are Tiled, LDtk, similar tools charging?
3. **Finance + Marketing:** Model 3 pricing scenarios (low/mid/high) with revenue and adoption impact
4. **Legal:** Review any pricing change for ToS implications (existing users, grandfathering)
5. **Orchestrator:** Synthesize into decision brief → `/templates/decision-brief.md`
6. **Founder:** Decides

## Pricing Principles (current hypothesis)
- Indie solo devs are price-sensitive — freemium with AI feature paywall likely optimal
- AI features are the value driver — free tier should demonstrate AI value, not hide it
- Annual pricing preferred for cash flow predictability

## Anti-Patterns
- Don't change pricing without modeling the impact on existing users
- Don't price AI features separately from the core tool (complexity kills conversion)
- Don't benchmark only against dev tools — also benchmark against game-specific tools (Tiled: $0, LDtk: donation-ware)
