2. Strategic foundation (Strategy)

2.1 North star

Help solo developers and small teams ship high-quality 2D metroidvanias and platformers without losing months to disconnected tooling. Defensibility is the integrated pipeline (sprites → rooms → world graph → export) plus AI that proposes structured output for human approval—not silent mutation of authored content.

2.2 Tool + game flywheel

  • Proof by production: Ashen Hollow demonstrates that the Workbench produces shippable game content, not toy demos.
  • Compounding infrastructure: Workbench investment accelerates every future title in the game library.
  • Credible narrative: “Built by game developers, for game developers” requires evidence; the game library is the evidence.

2.3 Platform choice (web-first)

Strategic intent: Browser delivery reduces install friction, enables shareable URLs and embeddable demos, and reaches users on locked-down machines. Tradeoffs (offline, filesystem, performance ceilings) are engineering problems to mitigate—not reasons to abandon the acquisition advantage without a deliberate Strategy decision.

2.4 Assumptions to monitor

  • Demand for AI-native creative workflows in indie gamedev continues to grow.
  • Model capability for spatial / layout assistance improves faster than commodity “full auto level gen” commoditizes trust.
  • Differentiation remains integrated workflow + human-directed AI, not a single-feature demo.

Revisit quarterly with Strategy if a major platform ships a directly comparable integrated toolchain or if AI level-gen positioning floods the narrative.

4. Audience & jobs-to-be-done (Marketing)

4.1 Core job (JTBD)

Users are not hiring the product to “place tiles.” They are hiring it to ship a game they are proud of without getting stuck in tooling for months. Three dimensions must appear in serious messaging:

DimensionJobMessage hook
FunctionalCorrect structure, reliable exports, sane data for engine import.“Structured rooms and worlds you can trust in your pipeline.”
EmotionalFeel competent and in control—not dependent on opaque AI.“AI-assisted, human-directed. Nothing applies until you say so.”
SocialShow work-in-progress with pride; pass the “not slop” sniff test.“Devlog-friendly: real UI, real iteration, real craft.”

4.2 Adoption lifecycle

Now — early adopters: Technically sophisticated; tolerate rough edges; value authenticity and visible iteration over glossy promises.

Next — early majority: Need reliability, documentation, proof, and polish. Marketing must not optimize conversion before this group has reasons to trust.

4.3 Anti-personas (do not optimize for)

  • Builders who want fully automated world generation with no authorship—that is a different product category.
  • Audiences who expect guaranteed playable AI output; we explicitly do not promise that.

5. Positioning & messaging architecture

5.1 Pillars (use in decks, site, long posts)

  1. Integrated pipeline — Sprite, room, and world authoring connected for metroidvania-scale projects.
  2. Human-directed AI — Suggestions and drafts; explicit review before data hits your files.
  3. Proof by shipping — We eat our own cooking: Ashen Hollow and future library titles.
  4. Web-native workflow — Low friction to try; shareable; no install cliff for many users.

6. Voice, tone & DevRel (Marketing)

6.1 Voice principles

  • Practitioner-first: First-person or small-team perspective; concrete technical detail beats slogans.
  • Honest limits: Show wrong Copilot suggestions and fixes—credibility beats polish theater.
  • Give before take: Standalone insights on metroidvania design, room graphs, or AI workflow earn attention; pure pitches do not.
  • No fake corporate “we’re thrilled to announce”: Indie devs filter that out instantly.

6.2 Language guardrails (2026)

7. Claims, AI & legal discipline

7.1 Non-negotiable “never claim”

  • Features not shipped or not reliable in production.
  • Deterministic “always playable” AI output.
  • Pricing, tiers, or refunds without Founder + Finance/Legal.
  • That AI replaces authorship or silently changes game runtime without user action.
  • Probabilistic AI behavior described as guaranteed.

7.2 Engineering sign-off rule

Any public claim touching exports, Copilot behavior, performance, or browser support should be traceable to shipped behavior or labeled as experimental. When in doubt, Marketing runs the positioning-review action from the Marketing charter before publish.

7.3 Product-led growth hooks

  • First-session win: First meaningful export or saved room as the activation story—not just “signed up.”
  • Shareability: Layout screenshots and Copilot clips should look recognizably “real tool,” not mockups.

8. Visual identity (Design)

8.1 Design philosophy (summary)

  • Dark precision-tool aesthetic — professional creative software, not marketing landing page noise.
  • Single cyan accent (#00e8c8) for active, selected, focused interactive states.
  • Gold gradient reserved for primary CTA and select marketing emphasis—not general buttons.
  • Opacity layers preferred over heavy shadows; neutral shadows only (black rgba).
  • No light mode in product UI; do not introduce light-mode building blocks.

8.4 Spacing, radius, motion (non-optional rules)

  • Spacing: Multiples of 4px only; established exceptions (e.g. 10px button padding) per STYLE_GUIDE.
  • Radii: Use token scale (--radius-tight 14px for buttons/inputs; --radius-card 18px for panels; --radius-full for pills).
  • Motion: 120ms / 200ms; ease or ease-out; hover lift translateY(-1px) max; never transition: all.
  • Canvas: image-rendering: pixelated on pixel art surfaces.
  • Accessibility: WCAG 2.2 AA target; visible :focus-visible; min hit targets 44px standard / 28px compact toolbar; do not use color as sole state differentiator.

9.2 Ashen Hollow vs Workbench

Do not conflate in-game mood, lore, and environmental palette with toolchain UI tokens. Game marketing may be atmospheric and narrative-forward; tool marketing must stay precise and restrained. Cross-reference the art bible for in-world direction; cross-reference STYLE_GUIDE for UI.

Practical rule: Screenshots of Ashen Hollow gameplay sell the dream; screenshots of the Workbench sell the workflow. Label which is which in posts.

10. Marketing & splash surfaces

Hero pages may use layered gradients and ambient cyan/gold radial highlights per STYLE_GUIDE §12, still composed from approved tokens. Marketing CTAs may use slightly taller min-height (e.g. 46px) but same motion and radius discipline.
