1. Purpose of this document

This charter defines how the company presents itself: strategic north star, brand promise, audience, voice, visual alignment, and competitive framing. It is the single reference for founders, product, marketing, and design when making naming, messaging, and ship decisions.

Scope. The MV business operates two reinforcing streams: a browser-based Workbench (sprite animation, room layout, world-building tools with human-directed AI assistance) and a game library led by Ashen Hollow, a dark-fantasy metroidvania that proves the pipeline in production.

2. Strategic foundation

North star

Help solo developers and small teams ship high-quality 2D metroidvanias and platformers without getting stuck in tooling for months. The moat is an integrated pipeline (sprites, rooms, world graph, export) plus AI that proposes, never silently mutates—validated by games we ship ourselves.

Tool + game flywheel

  • Proof by production: Ashen Hollow is living evidence that the Workbench produces shippable content.
  • Compounding toolchain investment: every improvement speeds future titles in the game library.
  • Credible narrative: “Built by game developers, for game developers” requires proof; the game is the proof.

Assumptions to protect

Strategy rests on: a growing market for AI-native creative tools; improving model spatial reasoning for layout assistance; web-first delivery as an acquisition advantage; and differentiation through integrated workflow rather than generic “AI levels.” Revisit quarterly if major platforms ship a directly comparable integrated offering.

3. Brand promise

Functional: Reliable exports, clear validation, and structured data you can trust in your engine.

Emotional: You stay in control—AI assists and proposes; you approve.

Social: Your process is worth showing—devlogs, clips, and layouts read as craft, not “slop.”

Line we own: “AI-assisted, human-directed metroidvania tooling—from pixels to rooms—proven by the games we ship.”

4. Positioning & differentiation

LDtk Strong 2D/editor UX; indie adoption Manual-first; no unified AI Copilot or cross-tool pipeline in one product story.

Aseprite Category leader for pixel art Art creation, not full layout/world workflow or room-level AI assistance.
Emerging “AI level gen” Novel automation narratives Full automation is a different job; MV competes on suggestion + review, not replacement of authorship.

Language guardrails (2026): Prefer “AI-assisted,” “suggests,” “proposes,” “review and apply.” Avoid “AI-powered magic,” guaranteed outputs, or implying silent AI mutation. The indie community rewards precision and punishes vapor.

6. Audience & lifecycle

Now — early adopters: Technically curious indies who tolerate rough edges for capability; authenticity and visible craft beat polished empty claims.

Next — early majority: Solo/small teams shipping commercial platformers/metroidvanias; need reliability, documentation, proof, and polish. Premature conversion optimization before community trust is wasted effort.

Channels: itch.io devlogs, short screen captures (GIF/video), honest iteration posts, and community participation before promotion. Answer-engine-friendly docs and structured technical writing support discovery.

7. Visual & verbal identity (Design alignment)

Public-facing materials for MV tools should align with the canonical STYLE_GUIDE.md tokens:

  • Colors: Near-black backgrounds (#050709 family); single cyan accent (#00e8c8) for active/focus; warm gold reserved for primary CTA gradients where used.
  • Type: Bebas Neue for display/titles; Plus Jakarta Sans for UI and body; DM Mono for code, coordinates, and data.
  • Motion & shape: Generous radii for panels; subtle motion (120ms/200ms); hover lift max 1px; no “corporate generic” gradients or off-palette accents.
  • Voice: Founder-practitioner tone—specific, humble about limitations, excited about craft. No anonymous “marketing department” voice.

9. What we will not claim

  • Features not shipped or not reliable in production.
  • Deterministic “always playable” AI output—Copilot is assistive and reviewed.
  • Pricing or tiers without founder approval.
  • Replacement of human authorship—we sell leverage for authors, not autopilot creativity.
